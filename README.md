# Customer Behavior Analysis

## Executive Summary

This project focuses on analyzing customer transaction and behavioral data to identify customer segments, purchase patterns, and churn risks. The analysis was performed using Python in Google Colab with data preprocessing, exploratory data analysis, customer segmentation, and machine learning techniques.

The objective was to understand customer purchasing behavior, identify high-value and at-risk customers, and provide actionable recommendations that can help Alfido Tech improve customer engagement and retention. RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis and clustering techniques were used to segment customers into meaningful groups.

The results revealed distinct customer segments, purchasing trends, and churn indicators that can support data-driven business decisions and targeted marketing strategies.

## Dataset Information

Dataset: Customer Behavior Analysis Dataset

Source: Kaggle

The dataset contains customer transaction and behavioral information used to analyze purchasing patterns, customer value, and retention trends.

## Project Objectives

* Perform data cleaning and preprocessing.
* Conduct feature engineering for customer analysis.
* Segment customers using RFM analysis and clustering techniques.
* Identify purchase patterns and retention trends.
* Detect customers with high churn risk.
* Provide actionable recommendations for improving customer engagement.

## Methodology

### 1. Data Cleaning

* Checked for missing values.
* Removed duplicate records.
* Converted date fields into proper datetime format.
* Verified data consistency.

### 2. Feature Engineering

* Calculated Recency, Frequency, and Monetary (RFM) metrics.
* Derived customer purchase frequency.
* Calculated customer spending patterns.

### 3. Exploratory Data Analysis

* Analyzed customer purchase behavior.
* Examined revenue distribution.
* Studied product category performance.
* Visualized purchasing trends.

### 4. Customer Segmentation

* Applied RFM analysis.
* Grouped customers into meaningful segments such as Champions, Loyal Customers, Regular Customers, and At-Risk Customers.
* Performed clustering analysis to identify customer groups.

### 5. Churn Risk Analysis

* Identified customers with long periods of inactivity.
* Classified customers according to churn risk levels.

## Key Findings

* A small group of customers contributed a large portion of total revenue.
* Loyal customers showed higher purchase frequency and spending.
* Several customers displayed inactivity patterns indicating potential churn risk.
* Certain product categories generated significantly higher sales than others.
* Customer segmentation enabled targeted engagement strategies.
